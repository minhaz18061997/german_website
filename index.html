💡 80/20 Principles

  • The top 1,000 German words cover ~85% of spoken language. Learn vocabulary first.
  • At A1–A2, consume more than you produce. Input builds fluency.
  • Speak from day 1. Mistakes are how you improve.
  • 45–60 min/day consistently beats a 5-hour weekend session.
  • Pick ONE YouTube channel, ONE podcast, ONE grammar source. Go deep, not wide.
  • Use Anki for spaced repetition of vocabulary — 10 min/day is enough.

🔧 Recommended Tools

  • Anki — Free spaced repetition flashcards. Import vocabulary from the Vocabulary page.
  • iTalki — Affordable German tutors and language exchange partners.
  • Language Reactor — Dual subtitles on Netflix/YouTube with instant word lookup.
  • Tandem — Free language exchange with native German speakers.
  • Leo.org — Best German–English dictionary for learners.
  • DeepL — Best AI translation tool for checking full sentences.
